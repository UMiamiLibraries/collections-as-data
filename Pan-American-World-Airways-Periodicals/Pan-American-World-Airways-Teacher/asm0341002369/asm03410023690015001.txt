ROUND-THE-WORLD
•	HIGH SCHOOL
n the fall of 1959, five teachers and 37 students will depart from New York City for a trip around the world which will comprise also the senior year (or a post-graduate year) of accredited high school studies.
The International School of America has been organized to provide an opportunity for students who would like to study and travel around the world as the senior year in high school, or as a year between high school and college. The school’s Executive Director is Karl Jaeger. Its trustees include Dana M. Cotton, Secretary of the Harvard Graduate School of Education, Alexander J. Stoddard, Consultant to the Fund for the Advancement of Education, and others. Transportation is via Flying Clippers.
In the instructional program are included standard senior high school subjects, including humanities, social science, biology, French and mathematics. Students will come from various parts of the United States and from the world, including Japan, Egypt, France, England and other countries. The school will be co-educational, and its staff will include three men and two women. Cost is $4,650., which includes the tuition, transportation fares, lodging and food, text books, and incidental expenses for programmed activities, such as entrance fees to museums etc.
